5 Best Travel Credit Cards for Europe in 2024: Save Fees & Earn Miles

Stop losing money on foreign fees. These five travel credit cards will save you hundreds on your next European trip.

5 min read Travel Cards

Why You Need a Travel Card for Europe

I've been burned by foreign transaction fees. On a $5,000 European trip, that's $150 down the drain. Travel credit cards eliminate this. Plus, they earn you miles for flights and offer perks like travel insurance. In 2024, the right card can save you $200–$400 per trip.

1. Chase Sapphire Preferred – Best All-Rounder

Annual fee: $95. Foreign transaction fee: $0. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 points after $4,000 spend. Points transfer 1:1 to airlines like United and British Airways. I used this card for a Paris trip—earned enough for a free round-trip flight. Plus, it includes primary rental car insurance.

2. Capital One Venture X – Best for Lounge Access

Annual fee: $395. Foreign transaction fee: $0. Sign-up bonus: 75,000 miles after $4,000 spend. You get Priority Pass lounge access. At London Heathrow, lounge entry costs $40–$60 per visit. With 10 trips, you break even. Miles are worth 1 cent each for statement credits. I redeemed 50,000 miles for a $500 hotel stay in Rome.

3. American Express Gold – Best for Dining

Annual fee: $250. Foreign transaction fee: $0. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 points after $6,000 spend. Earn 4x points at restaurants worldwide. On a two-week trip to Barcelona, I earned 8,000 points just from tapas. Points transfer to Delta and Air France. Use the $120 dining credit to offset the fee.

4. Bank of America Travel Rewards – Best No-Fee Option

Annual fee: $0. Foreign transaction fee: $0. Sign-up bonus: 25,000 points after $1,000 spend. Points are worth 1 cent each for travel purchases. No annual fee means you keep it forever. I used this as a backup card for cash withdrawals in Prague—no extra costs.

5. Citi Premier – Best for Gas & Groceries

Annual fee: $95. Foreign transaction fee: $0. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 points after $4,000 spend. Earn 3x points on gas and groceries. Road-tripping through Italy? This card adds up. Points transfer to JetBlue and Virgin Atlantic. I saved $120 on a rental car in Tuscany using the card's travel protection.

Pro Tips for Using Cards in Europe

Always pay in local currency—never let the merchant convert to dollars. That's a 3–5% fee. Use a chip-and-PIN card for train stations and gas pumps. Many US cards are chip-and-signature, which can fail at unattended kiosks. I recommend carrying a backup like the Schwab debit card (no ATM fees).
