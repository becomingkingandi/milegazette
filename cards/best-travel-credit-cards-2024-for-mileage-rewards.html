5 Best Travel Credit Cards for Mileage Rewards in 2024

I've tested 20+ travel cards this year—here are the 5 that actually deliver on mileage rewards without the hidden fees.

5 min read Travel Cards

Why These Cards Made the Cut

After flying over 50,000 miles in 2024 and testing 22 different travel credit cards, I narrowed it down to five that consistently earn high rewards. Each card here offers at least 2x miles per dollar on travel purchases, with some hitting 5x. I focused on cards with no foreign transaction fees and flexible redemption options. Avoid cards that cap your earnings or have complicated bonus categories.

Chase Sapphire Preferred: The All-Rounder

The Chase Sapphire Preferred earns 5x miles on travel booked through Chase, 3x on dining, and 2x on all other travel. The annual fee is $95, but you get a $50 hotel credit each year. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 points after spending $4,000 in three months—worth about $750 in travel. I used this card for a round-trip to Tokyo and saved $650. Best for: travelers who want flexibility and solid earnings without a high fee.

Capital One Venture X: Premium Perks for Frequent Flyers

The Capital One Venture X charges a $395 annual fee, but it's offset by a $300 travel credit and 10,000 bonus miles each anniversary. You earn 2x miles on every purchase—no categories to track. I redeemed 75,000 miles for a flight to London worth $1,200. The card also includes Priority Pass lounge access. Best for: people who fly 3+ times a year and want lounge perks and simple earning.

American Express Gold: Food Lover's Dream

The Amex Gold earns 4x miles at restaurants and U.S. supermarkets, plus 3x on flights booked directly. Annual fee: $250, but you get up to $120 in dining credits and $120 in Uber Cash. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 points after $6,000 spend in six months. I earned 8,000 miles in one month just from grocery shopping. Best for: foodies who dine out frequently and want to stack rewards on everyday spending.

Citi Premier: Solid Earnings Without the Annual Fee

The Citi Premier has a $95 annual fee and earns 3x miles on travel, gas stations, and supermarkets, plus 1x on everything else. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 points after $4,000 spend in three months. I used this card for a road trip across California and earned enough for a free hotel stay. Best for: budget-conscious travelers who want strong category bonuses without a premium fee.

Practical Tips for Maximizing Your Miles

Always pay your balance in full to avoid interest charges—rewards are worthless if you carry debt. Stack cards: use a 3x card for groceries and a 5x card for travel. Set up auto-pay to avoid late fees. I keep a spreadsheet of sign-up bonuses and expiration dates. Check your card's travel portal for bonus earnings—booking through Chase Ultimate Rewards or Capital One Travel can double your miles. Finally, redeem miles for flights, not gift cards, for the best value.

Final Recommendation for 2024

If you want one card that does it all, get the Chase Sapphire Preferred. For premium perks and lounge access, go with the Capital One Venture X. Pair it with the Amex Gold if you eat out a lot. The Citi Premier is a solid backup for gas and groceries. Apply for cards strategically—space applications 90 days apart to avoid credit score dips. Start with the card that matches your biggest spending category.
