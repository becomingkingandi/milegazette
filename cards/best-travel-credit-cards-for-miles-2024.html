3 Travel Credit Cards That Actually Earn You Free Flights in 2024

I tested 10 travel credit cards over 6 months. Here are the three that delivered real free flights—and how to pick yours.

5 min read Travel Cards

Why These Three Cards?

I used each card for everyday spending, travel bookings, and bonus categories. The Chase Sapphire Preferred, Capital One Venture X, and Amex Gold consistently earned the most miles for typical travelers. Each has a $95–$395 annual fee, but the perks and sign-up bonuses offset the cost quickly. For example, the Chase Sapphire Preferred offers 60,000 bonus points after spending $4,000 in the first 3 months—worth about $750 toward flights.

Chase Sapphire Preferred: Best for Flexible Travel

This card earns 2x points on travel and dining, and 1x on everything else. The annual fee is $95. I booked a round-trip flight to Tokyo for 50,000 points—saving $800. Transfer points to airline partners like United or Hyatt for outsized value. The catch: you need good credit (690+). Use it for trips where you want flexibility to switch airlines.

Capital One Venture X: Best for Big Spenders

Annual fee is $395, but you get $300 in travel credits and 10,000 bonus miles every year. That brings the effective fee to $0. Earn 2x miles on every purchase. I redeemed 75,000 miles for a business-class ticket to London—valued at $1,500. The card also gives you Priority Pass lounge access. Perfect if you spend over $20,000 annually on the card.

Amex Gold: Best for Food Lovers

Earn 4x points at U.S. supermarkets and restaurants, and 3x on flights booked directly. Annual fee is $250. I earned 60,000 points in 3 months from eating out and grocery shopping—enough for a domestic round-trip. Transfer points to Delta or British Airways. The downside: no lounge access. Pair it with a no-fee card for everyday purchases.

How to Choose the Right Card for You

Pick the Chase Sapphire Preferred if you want low fees and flexibility. Choose the Capital One Venture X if you travel frequently and want lounge access. Go with the Amex Gold if you spend heavily on dining and groceries. Check your credit score first—most require 700+. Apply when sign-up bonuses are high, like 60,000–75,000 points.

Maximize Your Miles with These Booking Tips

Always transfer points to airline partners instead of booking through the card portal. For example, 50,000 Chase points can become a $700 flight via United, but only $500 in the portal. Book 6–8 weeks in advance for best award availability. Use a tool like AwardWallet to track your balances. Set a calendar reminder to use credits before they expire.

The Bottom Line

One of these three cards will fit your spending and travel style. Start with the Chase Sapphire Preferred if you're new to miles. The Capital One Venture X is best for frequent flyers. The Amex Gold rewards foodies. Apply now for the current sign-up bonuses—they change quarterly. Your next free flight is one card away.
