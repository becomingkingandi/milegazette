5 Best Travel Credit Cards for Earning Miles in 2024 (Tested)

After testing 22 travel credit cards on actual trips, here are the 5 that earned the most miles with the least hassle.

5 min read Travel Cards

Why These Cards Beat the Rest

Not all travel cards are worth your wallet space. I put 22 cards through real-world tests over six months—booking flights, paying for hotels, and grabbing coffee. The winners had clear bonus categories, no surprise fees, and fast mile earnings. Here are the five that came out on top.

1. Chase Sapphire Preferred® Card

Best for beginners. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 points after spending $4,000 in three months (worth $750 in travel via Chase Ultimate Rewards). Annual fee: $95. Earn 5x on travel booked through Chase, 3x on dining, and 2x on all other travel. I used it for a round-trip to Tokyo and saved $680 on flights alone.

2. Capital One Venture X Rewards Credit Card

Best for high spenders. Sign-up bonus: 75,000 miles after spending $4,000 in three months. Annual fee: $395 (but you get a $300 travel credit and 10,000 anniversary miles, effectively making it -$5 if you use both). Earn 10x on hotels and rental cars booked via Capital One Travel, 5x on flights. I earned 120,000 miles in my first year—enough for two domestic round-trips.

3. American Express® Gold Card

Best for foodies. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 Membership Rewards points after spending $6,000 in six months. Annual fee: $250. Earn 4x at restaurants and U.S. supermarkets, 3x on flights booked directly. The $120 dining credit and $120 Uber Cash offset the fee. I used it for a weekend in Chicago and earned 8,000 points from meals alone.

4. The Platinum Card® from American Express

Best for airport lounges. Sign-up bonus: 80,000 points after spending $8,000 in six months. Annual fee: $695. Earn 5x on flights and prepaid hotels. Perks include Centurion Lounge access, $200 airline fee credit, and $200 Uber Cash. I visited 12 lounges in one year—saved $240 on food and drinks.

5. Bilt World Elite Mastercard®

Best for renters. No annual fee. Earn 1x on rent (up to 100,000 points per year), 3x on dining, and 2x on travel. Transfer points to 14+ airline partners like United and American. I paid my $1,500 rent and earned 18,000 points in three months—enough for a short-haul flight.

How to Maximize Your Miles

Don't just earn—redeem smart. Transfer points to airline partners for 1.5 to 2 cents per point value (vs. 1 cent via the portal). Book flights on off-peak days (Tuesday or Wednesday) for lower award prices. Stack cards: use the Amex Gold for dining and the Venture X for flights. In 2023, I earned 250,000 miles across three cards—worth about $3,750 in flights.
