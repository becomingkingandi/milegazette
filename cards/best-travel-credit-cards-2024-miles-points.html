5 Travel Credit Cards That Actually Earn You Free Flights in 2024

Stop paying full price for flights. Here are the cards that put free travel in your pocket.

5 min read Travel Cards

Why Your Wallet Needs a Travel Card

I've flown to 30 countries in the last three years, and I've paid cash for exactly zero of those flights. A good travel credit card is the difference between budgeting for a trip and just booking one. The key is matching the card to your spending. If you fly Delta twice a year, a Delta card makes sense. If you're a free agent, go for flexible points like Chase Ultimate Rewards or Amex Membership Rewards.

Chase Sapphire Preferred: The All-Rounder

This is the card I recommend most often. Annual fee is $95 (waived first year). You get 5x points on travel purchased through Chase, 3x on dining, and 2x on all other travel. The sign-up bonus is typically 60,000 points after spending $4,000 in three months. That's worth $750 toward flights when transferred to partners like United or Hyatt. No foreign transaction fees.

Capital One Venture X: Premium Without the Sticker Shock

The $395 annual fee sounds steep, but here's the math: you get a $300 annual travel credit and 10,000 bonus miles every anniversary. That's $400 in value right there. You earn 2x miles on every purchase, and the sign-up bonus is 75,000 miles after spending $4,000 in three months. Lounge access included. I used this card to book a round-trip to Tokyo for $11.20 in taxes.

American Express Gold: For Foodies Who Fly

If you spend heavily on groceries and dining, this is your card. Annual fee is $250, but you get $120 in Uber Cash and $120 in dining credits annually. Earn 4x points at restaurants and U.S. supermarkets, and 3x on flights booked directly. The 60,000-point sign-up bonus (after $6,000 spend in six months) can get you a domestic round-trip plus a hotel night.

Citi Premier: The Underrated Earner

This card doesn't get enough love. Annual fee is $95. You earn 3x points on restaurants, supermarkets, gas stations, air travel, and hotels. That's broad coverage. Transfer points to partners like JetBlue or Virgin Atlantic. Sign-up bonus is 60,000 points after $4,000 spend in three months. I used this to book a business class seat to London for 50,000 points.

How to Pick the Right Card for You

Don't chase the biggest bonus. Look at where you spend. If you fly one airline exclusively, get their co-branded card. If you want flexibility, go with Chase or Capital One. Check the annual fee against your expected travel credits. And always pay your balance in full—interest kills the value. Start with one card, use it for everyday spending, and watch your miles stack up.

Final Tip: Stack with Booking Portals

Maximize your miles by booking through the card's travel portal when the price is right. For example, Chase Sapphire Preferred gives you 5x points on portal bookings. Combine that with a sale fare, and you're earning 10x or more. Just compare portal prices to direct booking first—sometimes the difference isn't worth it. Book smart, fly free.
