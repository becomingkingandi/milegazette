5 Travel Credit Cards That Will Skyrocket Your Mileage in 2024

Stop leaving free miles on the table. Here are the best travel credit cards for earning and redeeming miles in 2024, based on real-world testing.

5 min read Travel Cards

Why Your Current Card Is Costing You Miles

Most general cash-back cards earn just 1-2% back. A dedicated travel card can earn 3-5 miles per dollar on flights and hotels. Over a year of $10,000 in travel spending, that's 30,000-50,000 extra miles—worth a round-trip domestic flight or more. If you're not using a travel card, you're leaving free trips on the table.

The Chase Sapphire Preferred: Best All-Rounder

The Chase Sapphire Preferred offers a 60,000-point sign-up bonus after spending $4,000 in the first 3 months. That's worth $750 in travel via Chase Ultimate Rewards. Annual fee: $95, waived the first year. You earn 5x on travel booked through Chase, 3x on dining, and 2x on all other travel. I've used it for flights to Tokyo and hotels in Paris—redemption is seamless.

Capital One Venture X: Premium Perks, Lower Fee

The Capital One Venture X has a $395 annual fee, but you get a $300 travel credit and 10,000 bonus miles every anniversary. Net effective cost: $95. Sign-up bonus: 75,000 miles after spending $4,000 in 3 months. You earn 10x on hotels and rental cars booked through Capital One Travel, 5x on flights. I redeemed 50,000 miles for a flight to London—simple, no blackout dates.

American Express Gold: Best for Dining and Groceries

The Amex Gold card earns 4x points at restaurants and U.S. supermarkets (up to $25,000 per year), 3x on flights booked directly. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 points after $4,000 spend in 6 months. Annual fee: $250, but includes $120 in dining credits and $84 in Dunkin' credits. I earned 40,000 points in three months just from my weekly grocery runs. Transfer points to Delta or Air France for solid value.

Citi Premier: Strong for Everyday Spending

The Citi Premier card earns 3x points on gas, groceries, dining, and travel. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 points after $4,000 spend in 3 months. Annual fee: $95, not waived. Points transfer to 15+ airline partners, including Virgin Atlantic and Avianca. I transferred 30,000 points to Virgin for a flight to Dublin worth $600. It's a workhorse for daily expenses.

How to Choose the Right Card for Your Travel Style

If you fly Delta or stay at Marriott, consider the co-branded cards. For flexibility, Chase and Amex offer transfer partners. If you value simplicity, the Capital One Venture X is hard to beat. Stack cards: use Amex Gold for groceries, Chase Sapphire for travel bookings. Always check the annual fee against the perks—if you travel 2+ times a year, premium cards pay off.

Pro Tips for Maximizing Your Miles

Never pay with miles if you can earn more by paying cash and meeting a sign-up bonus. Book award flights early—many programs release seats 330 days out. Use airline transfer partners for better value; 50,000 points might get a $200 flight via the portal but a $800 flight via a partner. Finally, track your spending categories—one card for dining, another for travel can double your earnings.
