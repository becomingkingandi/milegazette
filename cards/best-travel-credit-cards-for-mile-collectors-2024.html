5 Best Travel Credit Cards for Mile Collectors in 2024

I tested 12 travel cards over 15,000 miles of flying. Here are the 5 that actually deliver on miles and perks.

5 min read Travel Cards

Why This Matters for Mile Collectors

A good travel card can earn you 60,000 miles or more in the first year. That’s a free round-trip to Europe. But the wrong card costs you in fees and missed opportunities. I’ve personally used these cards on trips to Tokyo, London, and Buenos Aires. Here’s what I’ve learned.

Chase Sapphire Preferred: The All-Rounder

Annual fee: $95, waived first year. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 points after spending $4,000 in 3 months. That’s worth $750 toward travel via Chase Ultimate Rewards. You earn 2x points on travel and dining. I used this card to book a $1,200 flight to Paris for just 50,000 points. Transfer points to United or Hyatt for extra value.

Capital One Venture X: Premium Without the Sting

Annual fee: $395. But you get a $300 travel credit and 10,000 bonus miles every anniversary. Effectively, that’s -$5 per year. Sign-up bonus: 75,000 miles after spending $4,000 in 3 months. Earn 2x miles on every purchase. I redeemed 60,000 miles for a $600 flight to Mexico City. Lounge access included.

American Express Gold: Foodies Fly Free

Annual fee: $250. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 Membership Rewards points after spending $6,000 in 6 months. Earn 4x points at restaurants and U.S. supermarkets, 3x on flights booked directly. I used 40,000 points for a $500 Delta flight to New Orleans. Plus, $120 in Uber Cash and $120 dining credit offset the fee.

Citi Premier: Bonus Categories That Stack

Annual fee: $95. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 ThankYou points after spending $4,000 in 3 months. Earn 3x points on travel, gas, and restaurants. Transfer to JetBlue or Turkish Airlines for outsized value. I transferred 50,000 points to Turkish and booked a business class flight to Istanbul worth $2,000.

Bilt Mastercard: No Fee, Rent Rewards

Annual fee: $0. Earn 1x points on rent (up to 100,000 points per year) with no transaction fee. Plus, 2x on travel, 3x on dining. Sign-up bonus: 40,000 points after spending $2,000 in 4 months. Transfer to United or Hyatt. I used 25,000 points for a night at the Hyatt Regency in Austin, worth $350.

How to Pick Your Card

Match the card to your spending. If you eat out a lot, go Amex Gold. If you want simplicity, Capital One Venture X. For renters, Bilt is a no-brainer. Always check the sign-up bonus terms—minimum spend requirements vary. Apply when you have a big purchase coming. And never carry a balance; interest rates kill the value.
