5 Best Travel Credit Cards of 2024: A Detailed Comparison

We tested the top travel credit cards on miles, perks, and fees—here's which one fits your trip best.

5 min read Travel Cards

Why Your Travel Card Matters

A good travel credit card can save you $500+ per year on flights and hotels. The wrong one costs you in fees and lost rewards. I've used over a dozen cards on trips from Tokyo to Tbilisi. Here are the five that actually deliver.

Chase Sapphire Preferred: Best All-Rounder

Annual fee: $95. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 points after $4,000 spend in 3 months (worth $750 toward travel). Points transfer 1:1 to United, Hyatt, and 11 other partners. I used this card for a week in Portugal—got free flights and three nights at a Hyatt in Lisbon. It also has primary rental car coverage, which saved me $200 on insurance alone.

Capital One Venture X: Best for Lounge Access

Annual fee: $395 (but includes a $300 travel credit and 10,000 anniversary miles, effectively making it $5 net). Sign-up bonus: 75,000 miles after $4,000 spend in 3 months. Unlimited 2x miles on every purchase. The Priority Pass membership gives you access to 1,300+ lounges. I used it at Istanbul Airport—free food, showers, and Wi-Fi saved me $60 in one layover.

American Express Gold: Best for Foodies

Annual fee: $250. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 Membership Rewards points after $6,000 spend in 6 months. Earn 4x points at restaurants and U.S. supermarkets, plus 3x on flights. The $120 dining credit and $120 Uber Cash offset the fee. I earned 8,000 points on a weekend in Paris just on meals. Transfer points to Delta or Air France for sweet spots.

Citi Premier: Best for Flexible Travel

Annual fee: $95. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 ThankYou points after $4,000 spend in 3 months. Earn 3x on gas, groceries, dining, and air travel. Points transfer to 15 partners, including JetBlue and Turkish Airlines. I booked a round-trip to Costa Rica for 30,000 points—saved $450. The $100 hotel credit is a nice bonus.

Bank of America Travel Rewards: Best No-Fee Option

Annual fee: $0. Sign-up bonus: 25,000 points after $1,000 spend in 90 days (worth $250). Earn 1.5x on all purchases. No foreign transaction fees. If you have a Bank of America account, Preferred Rewards members get a 25-75% bonus. I used this for a month in Mexico—no fees, and the points covered my flight home.

How to Pick the Right Card for You

Start with your travel habits. Fly United or stay at Hyatt? Get the Chase Sapphire Preferred. Want lounge access? Go Capital One Venture X. Eat out a lot? American Express Gold. Travel once a year? The Bank of America card is free and simple. Apply for one card at a time to protect your credit score. Most bonuses require good to excellent credit (690+). Use each card for everyday spending to hit the sign-up bonus fast.
