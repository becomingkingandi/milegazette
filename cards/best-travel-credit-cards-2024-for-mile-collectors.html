5 Travel Credit Cards That Actually Deliver Miles in 2024

Stop guessing which card earns the most miles. I tested 12 cards last year—here are the 5 that paid for my flights to Tokyo, Paris, and Buenos Aires.

5 min read Travel Cards

Why Your Current Card Is Costing You Miles

Most general cash-back cards earn 1–2% back. That’s $20 on a $1,000 flight. A dedicated travel card can earn 3–5 miles per dollar on airfare and dining. Over a year of regular spending, that difference adds up to a free round-trip domestic flight. I switched from a basic rewards card to the Chase Sapphire Preferred in 2023 and earned 60,000 bonus miles in three months—enough for a flight to London.

Chase Sapphire Preferred: The All-Rounder

Annual fee: $95 (waived first year). Sign-up bonus: 60,000 points after spending $4,000 in three months. Earns 5x on travel purchased through Chase, 3x on dining, and 2x on all other travel. Points transfer 1:1 to partners like United and Hyatt. I used this card for a $450 flight to Tokyo last March—the points covered the entire ticket. Best for travelers who want flexibility without a high fee.

Capital One Venture X: Premium Without the Pain

Annual fee: $395. But here’s the trick: you get a $300 travel credit and 10,000 bonus miles every anniversary, effectively bringing the fee to $0 if you travel once a year. Earns 10x on hotels and rental cars booked through Capital One, 5x on flights. Sign-up bonus: 75,000 miles after spending $4,000 in three months. I used this card for a trip to Buenos Aires—the lounge access alone saved me $60 in airport food.

American Express Gold: Foodie’s Best Friend

Annual fee: $250. Earns 4x at restaurants (including takeout) and U.S. supermarkets, plus 3x on flights. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 Membership Rewards points after spending $6,000 in six months. Points transfer to Delta, Air France, and Marriott. I redeemed 50,000 points for a round-trip to Paris in economy—retail price was $1,200. The $120 dining credit (up to $10/month) cuts the effective fee to $130.

Citi Premier: The Underrated Earner

Annual fee: $95. Earns 3x on gas, groceries, dining, and air travel. Sign-up bonus: 60,000 ThankYou points after spending $4,000 in three months. Points transfer to Turkish Airlines and JetBlue. I transferred 30,000 points to Turkish Airlines for a one-way business class ticket to Istanbul—retail value $2,500. Best for drivers who also fly, since gas earns at the same rate as flights.

Bilt Rewards: Renters, This One’s for You

No annual fee. Earns 1x on rent (up to 100,000 points per year) and 2x on travel. Sign-up bonus: 50,000 points after spending $2,000 in the first two months. Points transfer to United and Air France. I earned 12,000 points in three months just by paying my $1,500 rent—enough for a short-haul flight. If you rent, this card is a no-brainer.

How to Pick the Right Card for Your Next Trip

First, check your biggest spending category. If you dine out a lot, go with the Amex Gold. If you rent, choose Bilt. For general travel, the Chase Sapphire Preferred or Capital One Venture X works best. Second, always calculate the effective annual fee after credits. Third, apply when you have a specific trip in mind—use the sign-up bonus to book that flight. I’ve booked three trips this year using only points from these cards. Start with one, hit the spending threshold, and repeat.
