Lisbon on a Budget: How to Spend 48 Hours for Under $150

I spent 48 hours in Lisbon for $145—here's exactly how to do it without missing the best views, pastéis de nata, and fado.

5 min read Destinations

Why Lisbon?

Lisbon packs more value than any other Western European capital. You get Atlantic sunsets, cobblestone hills, and pastel buildings—all at prices 30-40% lower than Paris or London. I visited in March 2024 and spent $145 total on food, transit, and activities for a full weekend. That included a tram ride, a fado show, and more custard tarts than I'll admit.

Getting In and Around

Fly into Lisbon Portela Airport (LIS), just 20 minutes from the city center. A metro ticket costs €1.65 ($1.80) one-way. Skip the €20 taxis. For 48 hours, buy a Viva Viagem card (€0.50) and load a 24-hour pass for €6.80. That covers all metro, buses, and trams—including the famous Tram 28. Pro tip: ride it early (before 9 a.m.) to avoid crowds. Walking is free and faster for exploring Alfama and Bairro Alto.

Where to Stay Under $50 a Night

Skip the pricier Chiado district. Stay in Alfama or Graça for authentic vibes and lower rates. I booked a private room at Home Lisbon Hostel for €38 ($42) per night. It's clean, central, and includes a free walking tour. For a budget hotel, try Hotel Portugal (€55/night) near Rossio Square. Book direct or via Hostelworld for the best deals—avoid third-party markups.

Free and Cheap Sights

Lisbon's best views cost zero. Climb to Miradouro das Portas do Sol for a postcard shot of the Tagus River. Then walk to Miradouro da Graça for sunset—locals gather here with beer cans and guitars. The LX Factory market (Sundays only) has free entry and street art. Entry to Jerónimos Monastery is €10, but you can admire the exterior and nearby Belém Tower for free. Save money by picnicking in Jardim Botânico with supplies from the local Pingo Doce grocery store.

Eating Well for Under €15 a Day

Pastéis de nata are €1.20 each at Manteigaria in Chiado—skip the touristy Pastéis de Belém line. For lunch, grab a bifana (pork sandwich) at O Trevo for €3.50. Dinner? Head to Time Out Market for variety, but eat at the counter to avoid table service fees. I split a €9 plate of grilled sardines and a €2 glass of vinho verde at A Tasca do Zé. Total daily food cost: €12-15. Tip: drink tap water (it's safe and free) instead of bottled.

One Splurge Worth It: Fado

Fado is Lisbon's soul—don't skip it. A dinner show in Alfama runs €35-50, but you can catch a 30-minute performance at Clube de Fado for €15 (drink included). I went to a free fado session at Casa de Fado on a Tuesday night (check their Instagram for schedules). The music is raw and emotional. Bring cash for the hat passed around after—€2 is fine.

Final Tips for a Smooth Trip

Bring comfortable shoes—Lisbon is built on seven hills. Use the 28 tram for the steepest climbs, but walk down. Avoid eating on Rua Augusta (tourist traps). Instead, wander into side streets in Príncipe Real. For data, buy a prepaid SIM at the airport for €10 (5GB from Vodafone). And always carry a reusable water bottle—the city has free refill stations. With these tips, you'll leave Lisbon with full pockets and a full heart.
