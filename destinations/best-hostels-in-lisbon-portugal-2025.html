Best Hostels in Lisbon, Portugal (2025): Where to Stay for Under €40

I slept in 12 Lisbon hostels last year—here are the 7 that deliver clean beds, great locations, and real value without breaking your budget.

5 min read Destinations

Why Lisbon Hostels Beat Hotels for Most Travelers

Lisbon is one of Europe's most affordable capitals, but hotel prices have jumped 30% since 2022. Hostels solve this. Dorm beds run €18–€35 per night, and private rooms with en-suite bathrooms start around €70. Many include free breakfast, walking tours, and rooftop bars with views of the Tagus River. For solo travelers, hostels also offer instant community—group dinners, pub crawls, and day trips to Sintra or Cascais.

1. Yes! Lisbon Hostel (Alfama) – Best for First-Timers

Located in the heart of Alfama, a 5-minute walk from São Jorge Castle. Dorm beds from €22; private double with bathroom €85. Free breakfast includes fresh pastéis de nata. Rooftop terrace with sunset views. The staff runs a free walking tour daily at 10 AM. Book at least 2 weeks ahead for summer. Rating: 9.4/10 on Hostelworld.

2. Home Lisbon Hostel (Chiado) – Best for Nightlife

A 10-minute walk from Bairro Alto's bars. Dorm beds from €25; private twin €90. Includes a free family-style dinner every night at 8 PM (pasta, salad, wine). The hostel organizes pub crawls to Pink Street and LX Factory on Fridays. Earplugs recommended—the party vibe runs until 2 AM. Rating: 9.2/10.

3. Lisbon Destination Hostel (Baixa) – Best for Digital Nomads

Right off Rossio Square, steps from the metro. Dorm beds from €18; private room €65. Coworking space with fast Wi-Fi (200 Mbps), free printing, and lockers. Quiet hours after 10 PM. Breakfast costs €5 extra but includes fresh fruit. Popular with remote workers—book early for weekday stays. Rating: 8.8/10.

4. Sunset Destination Hostel (Cais do Sodré) – Best for Views

Overlooking the Tagus River, near Time Out Market. Dorm beds from €30; private double €95. Rooftop bar with live music on weekends. Free sangria hour from 6–7 PM. The hostel is a 2-minute walk from the ferry to Cacilhas (€1.35 one-way) for dinner at local seafood spots. Rating: 9.0/10.

5. Independente Hostel (Bairro Alto) – Best for Privacy

A converted 18th-century palace. Dorm beds from €35; private rooms from €80. No bunk beds in dorms—each bed is a capsule with privacy curtain, reading light, and USB port. Free walking tour of Bairro Alto. The bar is quiet; this is not a party hostel. Rating: 8.7/10.

Booking Tips for Lisbon Hostels

Book directly on the hostel's website for free cancellation and sometimes a 10% discount. Avoid third-party sites for summer stays—they often sell out and charge higher rates. Pack a padlock (most hostels charge €2–€3 for rentals). Bring earplugs if you're near Alfama's tram 28 route—the bells start at 6 AM. For late arrivals, confirm check-in times; many Lisbon hostels close reception at midnight.
