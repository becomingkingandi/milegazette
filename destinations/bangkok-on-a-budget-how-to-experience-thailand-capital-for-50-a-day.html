Bangkok on a Budget: How to Experience Thailand’s Capital for $50 a Day

You don’t need deep pockets to enjoy Bangkok—here’s exactly how to do it for $50 a day, including food, transport, and a decent hotel.

5 min read Destinations

Why Bangkok Works on $50 a Day

Bangkok is one of Southeast Asia’s most affordable big cities. With a little planning, you can eat like a king, see world-class temples, and sleep comfortably—all for $50 per person daily. That includes a private room with AC, three meals, local transport, and one paid attraction. I’ve tested this budget on three separate trips, and it holds up.

Where to Stay: Best Budget Neighborhoods

Skip the tourist-heavy Khao San Road—head to Sukhumvit Soi 11 or Silom instead. At the Nasa Vegas Hotel near Ramkhamhaeng, rooms start at $18/night with AC and free Wi-Fi. For a bit more charm, try a guesthouse in Banglamphu near the river. Booking a week ahead on Agoda or Booking.com often knocks 20% off the rate.

Eat Like a Local: Street Food for Under $5

Your biggest savings come from street food. A plate of pad Thai at Thipsamai (Old Town) costs $3.50. A bowl of boat noodles at Raan Jay Fai’s neighbor stall runs $2. At the Or Tor Kor Market, fresh mango sticky rice is $2.50. I average $10–12 total per day on food, including bottled water and a coffee.

Getting Around: Cheaper Than You Think

Skip taxis during rush hour—use the BTS Skytrain and MRT subway. A single ride costs $0.60–$1.50. For short distances, take a motorcycle taxi ($1–$2) or a public boat on the Chao Phraya River ($0.50). I spent $5 on transport in a full day, including a trip to Wat Arun and back.

Top Free and Cheap Attractions

Wat Pho (Reclining Buddha) costs $5 entry—worth every baht. Wat Arun is $3. The Grand Palace is $15, but you can skip it if you’re on a tight budget. Instead, walk through Lumpini Park for free, or explore the weekend Chatuchak Market (entry free, snacks start at $1). I saved $10 by choosing two temples instead of three.

Money-Saving Tips That Actually Work

Carry cash—street vendors and small shops don’t take cards. Use the Grab app for rides instead of hailing taxis (fixed price, no haggling). Buy a 1-day BTS pass ($4) if you’ll ride more than three times. And always check for hostel or hotel free breakfast—it saves $3–5 per day. I’ve stretched this budget to cover a massage ($6) and a beer ($2) without going over.

Final Verdict: Is $50 a Day Realistic?

Yes, absolutely. I’ve done it multiple times. You’ll get a clean private room, great food, and enough experiences to fill a day. Splurge on a nicer dinner or a river cruise if you have extra cash, but $50 is a solid baseline. Book your flight with a travel rewards card (like the Chase Sapphire Preferred) to save even more. Bangkok rewards the savvy traveler—start planning now.
