Lisbon on a Budget: 3 Days of Pastéis, Trams, and Sunsets for Under $150

I spent 72 hours in Lisbon and spent just $135 on food, transport, and attractions — here’s exactly how you can do it too.

5 min read Destinations

Why Lisbon Is a Budget Traveler’s Dream

Lisbon offers Western European charm at Southern European prices. A filling bifana sandwich costs €3.50. A ride on the iconic Tram 28 sets you back €3. And the best viewpoints — miradouros like São Pedro de Alcântara — are completely free. In three days, you can eat well, see the sights, and still have change from €150 if you skip the pricier tourist traps.

Day 1: Alfama, Pastéis, and a Sunset View

Start in the Alfama district. Wander the narrow alleys uphill to the Castelo de São Jorge (€10 entry, but the views from outside are just as good). Grab a pastel de nata at Manteigaria — €1.30 each, fresh and warm. For lunch, head to Time Out Market in Cais do Sodré. A seafood plate from Marlene Vieira costs around €12. End the day at Miradouro das Portas do Sol. Bring a €1.50 Super Bock beer from a corner shop and watch the sun dip over the Tagus River.

Day 2: Belém, Tram 28, and a Free Fado Show

Take the 15E tram to Belém (€1.50 single ticket). Visit the Jerónimos Monastery — entry is €10, but the exterior is free and stunning. Try the original pastéis de Belém (€1.20 each) at Antiga Confeitaria de Belém. Ride Tram 28 back through Graça — a full loop costs €3. In the evening, catch free fado at Tasca do Chico in Bairro Alto. Order a glass of vinho verde (€2.50) and tip the musicians a few euros.

Day 3: LX Factory, a Ferry Ride, and Cheap Seafood

Spend the morning at LX Factory, a creative hub under the 25 de Abril Bridge. It’s free to browse the shops and street art. Lunch at A Praça — a buffet with salads and grilled fish for €11. Afternoon: take the ferry from Cais do Sodré to Cacilhas (€1.25 each way). The view of Lisbon from the water is unbeatable. For dinner, head to Ramiro’s in Intendente for garlic shrimp and clams — a full meal runs about €20.

Where to Stay Without Breaking the Bank

Skip the pricier Chiado and Alfama hotels. Stay in Arroios or Anjos — neighborhoods with metro access and €25–€35 per night hostels. I booked a private room at Home Lisbon Hostel for €32 a night. It’s clean, central, and includes a free walking tour. For budget hotels, try Hotel Ibis Lisboa José Malhoa — rooms from €50 in low season.

Money-Saving Tips That Actually Work

Buy a Viva Viagem card (€0.50) and load it with €10 for unlimited metro, bus, and tram rides over 3 days. Eat at tascas (small taverns) for lunch — a prato do dia (daily plate) costs €7–€9. Avoid restaurants with multilingual menus near Praça do Comércio; they’re overpriced. Use the 737 bus instead of Tram 28 for the same route without the queue. And always carry cash — many small places don’t take cards.

The Bottom Line: Is It Worth It?

Yes. Lisbon delivers culture, food, and views at a fraction of the cost of Paris or London. My 3-day total: €122.50 (about $135). That covered all meals, transport, two paid attractions, and a ferry ride. With careful planning, you can stretch that budget even further. Book your flights midweek in shoulder season (April or October) for the best deals. Start your trip at Lisbon Portela Airport — metro to the city center costs €1.80 and takes 20 minutes.
