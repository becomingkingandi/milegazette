Bangkok Street Food: 7 Stalls That Beat Any Restaurant

I ate at 40 street stalls in Bangkok last month. These 7 are worth your time—and your baht.

5 min read Destinations

Why Bangkok Street Food Wins

Bangkok’s street food isn’t just cheap—it’s world-class. A plate of pad thai costs 50–80 baht ($1.40–$2.30). Compare that to a sit-down restaurant where the same dish runs 200 baht. The stalls use family recipes passed down for decades. You get flavor, not overhead.

1. Thipsamai (Pad Thai) – 80 Baht

On Maha Chai Road near the Golden Mount. This stall has been open since 1966. Their pad thai is wrapped in a thin egg crepe. Order the "Pad Thai Goong Ob" (shrimp wrapped in egg). Cash only. Lines move fast. Go at 5 PM to beat the rush.

2. Jay Fai (Crab Omelette) – 300 Baht

Michelin-starred, but still a street stall. On Maha Chai Road, same street as Thipsamai. Her crab omelette costs 300 baht ($8.50)—pricey for street food, but it’s packed with lump crab. Arrive by 9 AM to queue. No reservations. Bring cash.

3. Khao Man Gai Pratunam (Chicken Rice) – 50 Baht

On Soi Petchburi 30 in Pratunam. This stall serves the best chicken rice in Bangkok. Poached chicken, fragrant rice, and a side of broth. 50 baht ($1.40). Open 6 AM–2 PM. Go for breakfast. Eat at the plastic tables on the sidewalk.

4. Somtum Der (Papaya Salad) – 80 Baht

On Sri Rat Road near Silom. This stall specializes in som tam (green papaya salad). Order it with salted crab and sticky rice. 80 baht ($2.30). Spice levels: 1 (mild) to 5 (dangerous). I recommend level 3. Open 11 AM–8 PM.

5. Moo Satay (Grilled Pork Skewers) – 10 Baht Each

On Sukhumvit Soi 38, just off the main road. These skewers are grilled over charcoal, then served with peanut sauce and cucumber relish. 10 baht each ($0.28). Buy 10. Open 5 PM–midnight. Best paired with a cold Singha beer.

6. Khao Piak Sen (Lao Noodle Soup) – 60 Baht

On Charoen Krung Road near the River City mall. This stall serves a Lao-style chicken noodle soup. The broth is thick, rich, and loaded with herbs. 60 baht ($1.70). Add chili flakes and lime. Open 10 AM–6 PM. A hidden gem.

7. Raan Khao Niao Mamuang (Mango Sticky Rice) – 50 Baht

On Soi Rambutri near Khao San Road. This stall serves the best mango sticky rice in the old city. Sweet mango, warm sticky rice, and coconut cream. 50 baht ($1.40). Open 11 AM–9 PM. Go for dessert after a meal at any of the above.
