48 Hours in Bangkok: Best Temples, Street Food Stalls, and a Rooftop Bar

How to see Bangkok’s top temples, eat at three legendary street food stalls, and catch sunset from a rooftop bar in just two days.

5 min read Guides

Day 1 Morning: The Grand Palace and Wat Pho

Start at 8 AM sharp. The Grand Palace opens at 8:30 AM, but arriving early beats the crowds and heat. Entry fee is 500 THB ($14). Spend 90 minutes here—the Emerald Buddha and gold-plated structures are worth it. Walk five minutes to Wat Pho (entry 300 THB, $8.50) for the reclining Buddha. It’s 46 meters long. Expect 45 minutes inside.

Day 1 Lunch: Thipsamai for Pad Thai

Take a 10-minute taxi (around 80 THB, $2.25) to Thipsamai on Maha Chai Road. This stall has been serving pad thai since 1966. Order the classic pad thai wrapped in egg (150 THB, $4.25). It’s smoky, sweet, and tangy. Cash only. No reservations.

Day 1 Afternoon: Wat Arun and a Canal Boat

Cross the Chao Phraya River via ferry (15 THB, $0.40) to Wat Arun. Entry is 100 THB ($2.80). Climb the central prang for river views—steps are steep. Then take a 30-minute canal boat tour from Tha Tien pier: 60 THB ($1.70) per person. You’ll see stilt houses and local life.

Day 1 Evening: Sky Bar at Lebua

Head to Sky Bar on the 63rd floor of Lebua State Tower. Dress code: no shorts or flip-flops. Drinks start at 600 THB ($17). Order a Hango-over cocktail. Sunset is around 6:15 PM. Book a table if you want seats, but standing at the bar works fine. Views over the river are stunning.

Day 2 Morning: Chatuchak Weekend Market

If it’s Saturday or Sunday, take the BTS to Mo Chit station (45 THB, $1.30) and walk to Chatuchak Weekend Market. It’s open 9 AM to 6 PM. Over 15,000 stalls. Focus on sections 2 and 3 for vintage clothes, section 5 for home decor, and section 27 for food. Try the coconut ice cream (40 THB, $1.15). Bargain for prices—start at 50% of the asking price.

Day 2 Lunch: Jay Fai for Crab Omelet

Jay Fai on Maha Chai Road is a Michelin-starred street food spot. The crab omelet costs 1,000 THB ($28)—pricey for street food, but it’s packed with lump crab. Arrive by 9 AM to queue. Expect a 1-2 hour wait. Cash only. Bring a friend to hold your spot while you grab a drink at a nearby 7-Eleven.

Day 2 Afternoon: Jim Thompson House and Departure

End your trip at Jim Thompson House (entry 200 THB, $5.70). It’s a traditional Thai teak house with a silk museum. Tours run every 30 minutes. Allow 1 hour. Then take a taxi to Suvarnabhumi Airport (45 minutes, 400 THB, $11.30). Factor in 2 hours for security. You’ll make your flight.
