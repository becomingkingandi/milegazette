Tulum on a Budget: How to Visit for Under $50/Day in 2025

Tulum doesn't have to cost a fortune—here's how to experience its best beaches, ruins, and food for $50 a day or less.

5 min read Guides

Why Tulum Isn't Just for Instagram Influencers

Tulum's reputation as a luxury hotspot is overblown. I spent a week here last January and averaged $47/day. The trick? Skip the overpriced beach clubs and head to the public access points. Playa Paraíso is free, with soft sand and clear water. You'll pay $5 for a coconut from a vendor instead of $15 for a cocktail at a resort.

Where to Stay for Under $30/Night

Skip the hotel zone (rooms start at $150/night). Stay in Tulum Pueblo, the town center. I booked a private room at Che Tulum Hostel for $28/night—clean, with AC and a pool. For less, try Camping Tulum, where tents go for $12/night. Book two weeks ahead on Hostelworld to lock in rates.

Cheap Eats: Authentic Tacos for $2

Street food is your friend. Head to Antojitos La Chiapaneca on Calle Polar for $2 al pastor tacos. For breakfast, grab a $1.50 empanada at El Camello Jr. Avoid restaurants on the beach road—they charge $10 for a basic quesadilla. Grocery shop at Chedraui for snacks and water.

Free and Low-Cost Activities

The Tulum Ruins cost $4 (cash only, bring pesos). Go at 8 AM to beat crowds. Cenote Calavera is $7 and less touristy than Gran Cenote ($15). Bike rentals are $10/day from Tulum Bike Rental on Avenida Tulum—pedal to the beach in 15 minutes. Sunset at Playa Ruinas is free and stunning.

Getting Around Without a Rental Car

Skip the $50/day rental car. Take the ADO bus from Cancun Airport ($15, 2 hours). In town, use colectivos (shared vans) for $1 per ride. They run along the main road to the beach zone. I walked most days—Tulum Pueblo is compact and safe.

Money-Saving Tips from a Local

Exchange cash at the airport (rates are better than banks). Use ATMs in town, not at the beach (they charge $5 fees). Carry small bills—many vendors don't have change for $20. And skip the tour operators selling $50 cenote tours; you can visit three cenotes independently for $15 by bike.

The Bottom Line: $50/Day Breakdown

Here's my real budget: $28 for hostel, $8 for three meals, $10 for bike rental, $4 for ruins. Total: $50. Add $7 for a cenote if you skip the bike. Tulum's magic is in its raw beauty, not its price tags. With this plan, you'll leave with memories—and cash in your wallet.
