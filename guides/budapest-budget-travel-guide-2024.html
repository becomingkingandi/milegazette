Budapest on $50 a Day: How to See the Best of Hungary's Capital

Discover how to enjoy Budapest's thermal baths, ruin bars, and Danube views for less than $50 a day with these insider tips.

5 min read Guides

Why Budapest is a Budget Traveler's Dream

Budapest offers incredible value compared to other European capitals. A pint of beer costs around $2, and a hearty meal at a local eatery runs about $10. Public transport is efficient and cheap—a 24-hour pass is just $5. With affordable accommodation and free attractions like the Fisherman's Bastion, you can stretch your budget and still experience the city's charm.

Where to Stay: Best Budget Neighborhoods

Skip the tourist-heavy District V and head to District VII (the Jewish Quarter) for lively ruin bars and hostels. A dorm bed at a place like Maverick Hostel costs $15-20 per night. For a private room, try the Maverick City Lodge in District VI—rates start at $40. Both areas are walkable to major sites like the Parliament and Chain Bridge.

Cheap Eats: Local Favorites Under $10

Start your day at a bakery like Cserpes Tejivó for a pastry and coffee for $3. For lunch, hit a market like the Great Market Hall—try a langos (fried dough with sour cream) for $4. Dinner at a traditional restaurant like Gettó Gulyás serves hearty goulash for $8. Street food is king; grab a chimney cake from a stand for $2.

Free and Low-Cost Attractions

Walk the Buda Castle Hill for panoramic views, free of charge. Visit the House of Terror museum (entry $7) for a sobering history lesson. The Széchenyi Thermal Baths cost $20 for a day pass—worth it for the warm pools. Stroll along the Danube Promenade at sunset; it's free and stunning.

Getting Around: Public Transit Tips

Buy a 72-hour pass for $15 at metro stations or via the BudapestGO app. Trams 2 and 4/6 are scenic routes along the river. Avoid taxis; use the Bolt app for rides averaging $5 within the city. Walking is free and efficient for central districts.

Nightlife Without the Price Tag

Ruin bars like Szimpla Kert are iconic—grab a cheap beer ($2) and explore the eclectic decor. For live music, check out Instant for free entry most nights. Skip overpriced clubs; stick to District VII for affordable drinks and a local vibe.

Final Tips for Saving More

Book attractions online to skip queues and get discounts—like the Buda Castle funicular ($5 vs $7 at the gate). Use the Budapest Card for free transit and museum entry ($25 for 72 hours) if you plan to see many sites. Always carry cash for smaller vendors; cards are accepted at most places.
