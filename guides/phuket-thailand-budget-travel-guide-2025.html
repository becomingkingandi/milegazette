Phuket on a Budget: How to Visit Thailand’s Biggest Island for Under $50/Day

I spent a week in Phuket for $45 a day—here’s exactly how you can do it too, without missing the best beaches or local eats.

5 min read Guides

Where to Stay: Cheapest Neighborhoods

Skip Patong if you’re on a tight budget. Instead, stay in Kata or Karon Beach. A fan room in a guesthouse runs $12–$18/night on Booking.com. For a bit more comfort, try a basic bungalow in Rawai for $20–$25/night. Book 3 weeks ahead for the best rates.

Street Food: Eat Like a Local for $3–$5

Phuket’s street food scene is incredible. Hit the night market in Phuket Town for pad thai ($1.50), mango sticky rice ($2), and fresh spring rolls ($1). For a sit-down meal, try Raya Restaurant—curries run $4–$6. Avoid tourist-trap restaurants on Bangla Road; they charge triple.

Free and Cheap Beaches

Most beaches are free. Kata Noi and Nai Harn are quieter, with clean sand and public showers ($0.50). Rent a chair for $2–$3 or just lay your towel down. Avoid the paid sunbeds at Patong—they’re overpriced at $8.

Getting Around: Save on Transport

Rent a scooter for $7–$10/day from shops on Soi Ta-iad. You’ll need an international driving permit (IDP) to avoid fines. For short trips, use the local songthaew (shared truck) for $1–$2 per ride. Taxis from the airport to Kata cost $25—take the airport bus instead for $4.

Must-Do Activities Under $10

Visit the Big Buddha (free), hike to the Karon Viewpoint (free), or take a 2-hour Thai cooking class at Blue Elephant for $9. Snorkel at Coral Island—ferry costs $8 round-trip. Skip the overpriced elephant sanctuaries; choose ethical ones like Phuket Elephant Sanctuary for $50 (still a splurge, but worth it).

Money-Saving Tips from a Local

Withdraw cash from ATMs inside banks to avoid $3–$5 fees. Use 7-Eleven for cheap water ($0.30) and snacks. Haggle politely at markets—start at 50% of the asking price. And always check your hotel bill for hidden charges like “service fees” (some add 10%+).

Sample Budget Breakdown (1 Day)

Accommodation: $15. Food: $8 (2 meals + snacks). Transport: $3 (scooter rental). Activities: $5 (cooking class). Total: $31. Add a $10 buffer for drinks or tips, and you’re still under $50. This leaves room for one splurge, like a spa massage ($12) or a sunset cocktail ($5).
