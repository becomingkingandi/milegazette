The Best Time to Visit Portland, Oregon: A Seasonal Price & Crowd Guide

Visit Portland in spring or fall for the best balance of good weather, lower hotel rates, and fewer tourists.

4 min read Guides

Why Timing Matters in Portland

Portland doesn’t have a single “perfect” season—each one offers a different experience. I’ve visited in every season, and the difference in hotel prices and crowd sizes is dramatic. In summer, a downtown hotel can cost $250 a night. In winter, the same room drops to $120. Knowing when to go saves you real money and lets you enjoy the city without fighting for a table at Voodoo Doughnut.

Spring (March–May): The Sweet Spot

Spring is my top pick. The cherry blossoms at the Portland Japanese Garden peak in late March, and the weather is mild (50s–60s F). Hotel rates average $140–$170 per night. Crowds are thin, and you can snag a reservation at popular spots like Le Pigeon or Tasty n Alder without a two-week wait. Pack a rain jacket—April showers are real.

Summer (June–August): Peak Season, Peak Prices

Summer brings sunny skies and temps in the 70s–80s F, but it also brings crowds. Downtown hotels hit $200–$300 per night. The Portland Saturday Market is packed, and lines at Powell’s City of Books can stretch 15 minutes. If you must go in summer, book by February. Pro tip: skip the touristy food carts on SW 10th and head to Cartlandia on SE 82nd for shorter lines and better prices.

Fall (September–November): Harvest Season Bargains

Fall is a close second to spring. September is still warm (70s F), and hotel rates drop to $130–$160. The Oregon Wine Country harvest tours are in full swing—book a tasting at Domaine Drouhin for $25. By November, rain returns, but you’ll find hotel deals under $100. Visit the Portland Art Museum on a rainy afternoon for $20 admission.

Winter (December–February): The Budget Traveler’s Choice

Winter is cold and wet (40s F, steady rain), but it’s the cheapest time. Hotels average $100–$130 per night. The city is quiet, and you can explore the International Rose Test Garden (free) without crowds. Holiday events like the Portland Winter Light Festival (February) add sparkle. Pack waterproof boots—you’ll thank me.

Events That Affect Prices & Crowds

Avoid Portland Rose Festival (late May–June) if you hate crowds—hotels spike to $300+. The Oregon Brewers Festival (July) and Christmas market (December) also drive up rates. For lower prices, visit during non-event weeks. Check Travel Portland’s calendar before booking. I once saved $80 a night by shifting my trip one week earlier.

Final Verdict: Book Spring or Fall

For the best mix of weather, price, and space, target April–May or September–October. You’ll pay $130–$170 per night, see the city at a relaxed pace, and still enjoy outdoor attractions like Forest Park (free, 80 miles of trails). Use a travel credit card with no foreign fees—Portland is cash-friendly but not everywhere takes Amex. Book directly with hotels for cancellation flexibility.
