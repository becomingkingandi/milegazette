Budapest on $250: A 3-Day Guide to Thermal Baths, Ruin Bars, and Castle Views

See Budapest's highlights—thermal baths, ruin bars, and Castle Hill—for under $250, including accommodation and meals.

5 min read Guides

Why Budapest Fits a Tight Budget

Budapest is one of Europe's cheapest capitals. A pint of local beer costs $2, a hearty meal at a market hall runs $8, and public transit is $1.20 per ride. With $250, you cover accommodation, food, top attractions, and some nightlife. I spent three days here in 2023 and stuck to that budget without feeling pinched.

Day 1: Castle Hill and the Chain Bridge

Start at Buda Castle. Take bus 16 from Deák Ferenc tér for $1.20. The castle grounds are free. Walk down to Fisherman's Bastion for panoramic views—no entry fee. Cross the Chain Bridge on foot (free) to Pest. Lunch at Central Market Hall: grab a lángos (fried dough with sour cream, $3) or goulash soup ($5). Spend the afternoon exploring the market's stalls. Dinner at a local eatery like Gettó Gulyás, where a main dish costs $10. Total day one: $20.

Day 2: Thermal Baths and a Ruin Bar Crawl

Head to Széchenyi Thermal Bath in City Park. Entry is $18 for a day pass (book online to skip queues). Spend three hours in the outdoor pools—the 38°C main pool is a highlight. Lunch at the park's food trucks: a langos and soda for $6. Evening: visit ruin bars like Szimpla Kert. Entry is free, beers are $2.50. Try the ruin bar district on Kazinczy Street. Dinner at a ruin bar's kitchen, like Karavan Street Food, where a burger is $5. Total day two: $32.

Day 3: Parliament, River Cruise, and Jewish Quarter

Visit the Hungarian Parliament Building. Exterior views are free from Kossuth Square. Book a 45-minute tour inside for $10 (advance tickets recommended). Walk to the Shoes on the Danube Bank memorial (free). Take a 1-hour river cruise with Legenda for $12—best value for seeing both sides of the city. Lunch in the Jewish Quarter: try Macesz Huszár for a $7 falafel plate. Afternoon: explore the Great Synagogue (entry $8). Dinner at a local pizzeria like Pizzica for $8. Total day three: $45.

Where to Stay and How to Save on Transit

Stay in a hostel like Maverick Hostel in District VII (Jewish Quarter). Dorm beds start at $15 per night. For private rooms, check Airbnb near Deák Ferenc tér for $40 per night. Buy a 72-hour transit pass for $15—covers all buses, trams, and metro lines. Avoid taxis; use public transit or walk. Most attractions are within a 30-minute walk of each other.

Real Numbers: Your $250 Breakdown

Here's a sample budget: accommodation (3 nights hostel): $45. Food (9 meals): $72. Transit pass: $15. Attractions (bath, cruise, synagogue, tour): $48. Nightlife (4 beers, ruin bar entry): $10. Souvenirs and misc: $10. Total: $200. You'll have $50 left for extras like a fancy dinner or a spa treatment. Track spending with a free app like Trail Wallet to stay on budget.

Final Tips for First-Timers

Book thermal baths online to save $2–$3. Visit ruin bars early (6 PM) for fewer crowds. Use the Budapest Card only if you plan 3+ attractions—otherwise, pay-as-you-go is cheaper. Avoid restaurants on Váci Street (tourist trap pricing). Stick to local spots in District VII or near the market. Budapest is safe, but watch for pickpockets on tram 4-6. Enjoy the city's charm—it's one of Europe's best values.
